# Timepad --- Design Schema

## 1. Design Philosophy

Timepad is a personal sketchpad for your day --- calm, human, and
reflective rather than mechanical or corporate.\
The interface should evoke the experience of writing or sketching in a
notebook: approachable, personal, and non‑intrusive.

Emotional goals:

- Calm
- Honest
- Personal
- Non-judgmental
- Lightweight
- Reflective

---

## 2. Visual Identity

### Theme

Hand‑drawn / sketch‑inspired minimalism with subtle analog warmth.

Inspired by:

- Sketchbooks
- Paper notebooks
- Pencil drawings
- Soft ink lines
- Personal journals

---

## 3. Color System

### Core Palette

Background (Paper): **#F7F4EE**\
Primary Text (Ink): **#2B2B2B**\
Secondary Text: **#6E6A63**\
Dividers / Lines: **#E5E1D8**\
Accent (Muted Blue): **#5B7C99**

### Surfaces

Containers should use very light paper tones:

- Primary Surface: #FFFFFF
- Secondary Surface: #FBF9F5

### Status Colors (Muted)

- Productive: Dusty green tone
- Neutral: Soft gray
- Distracting: Muted warm tone (no harsh red)

Bright or saturated colors must be avoided.

---

## 4. Typography

### Font Pairing

Primary UI Font: **Geist**\
Decorative / Handwritten Font: **KG Teacher**

#### Usage Rules

KG Teacher is used sparingly for:

- Logo
- Major headings
- Empty states
- Friendly highlights

Geist is used for:

- Body text
- Controls
- Data
- Labels
- Navigation
- Numbers

Long passages must never use the handwritten font.

---

## 5. Layout Principles

### Spacious Design

- Generous whitespace
- Low visual density
- Clear separation between sections
- No crowded dashboards

### Card-Based Structure

Content is grouped into paper-like sections resembling sheets placed on
a desk.

---

## 6. Containers (Cards)

### Shape

- Soft rounded corners
- Clean edges
- No sharp corners
- Not overly bubbly

### Background

Light paper tone:

- #FFFFFF or #FBF9F5

### Border

- Thin, soft line
- Color: #E5E1D8
- Never dark or heavy

### Shadow

Very subtle depth --- like paper slightly lifted from a surface.

### Spacing

Generous internal padding so content can breathe.

---

## 7. Timeline Elements

Activity sessions appear as labeled strips representing parts of the
day.

Characteristics:

- Rounded rectangles
- Subtle category tint (not strong fills)
- Clear hierarchy of information
- Easy to scan quickly

---

## 8. Buttons

### Primary Button

- Rounded rectangle
- Background: #5B7C99
- Text: Light/white
- Clear but calm prominence

### Secondary Button

- Paper-like background
- Thin border (#E5E1D8)
- Dark text (#2B2B2B)

### Text Button

- Minimal appearance
- Accent-colored text (#5B7C99)
- Optional subtle underline on interaction

Buttons should feel like labeled paper tags, not glossy controls.

---

## 9. Input Fields

Designed to feel like writing on a form.

Shape:

- Soft rounded rectangle

Background:

- #FFFFFF or #FBF9F5

Border:

- Thin, color #E5E1D8

Focus State:

- Border changes to accent color #5B7C99
- No glow effects

---

## 10. Dividers

Used sparingly to separate content.

- Thin line
- Color: #E5E1D8
- Surrounded by whitespace
- Never heavy or dominant

---

## 11. Navigation

### Tabs

- Text-first design
- Subtle active indicator
- Accent color for active state (#5B7C99)
- No bulky tab shapes

Menus should feel quiet and unobtrusive.

---

## 12. Iconography

- Thin outline icons
- Rounded edges
- Minimal detail
- Sketch-compatible style

Colors:

- Default: Secondary text color
- Active: Accent color

---

## 13. Motion & Interaction

Animations must be subtle and purposeful.

Goals:

- Maintain calmness
- Provide gentle feedback
- Avoid distraction

Avoid:

- Bouncy motion
- Flashy transitions
- Gamified effects

---

## 14. Tone of Interface

Language should be:

- Neutral
- Supportive
- Non-judgmental
- Clear

Example:

Instead of: "You wasted time"\
Use: "Time spent on entertainment"

---

## 15. Privacy Communication

Privacy controls should be visible and reassuring.

Users must feel:

- In control
- Safe
- Free to pause tracking
- Able to remove data easily

---

## 16. Accessibility

- High readability
- Comfortable contrast
- Large touch targets
- Clear labeling
- Minimal reliance on color alone

---

## 17. Cross-Platform Consistency

The product should feel identical across devices through consistent:

- Colors
- Typography
- Terminology
- Layout logic
- Interaction patterns

---

## 18. Emotional Outcome

After using Timepad, users should feel:

- Informed, not judged
- Calm, not pressured
- Aware, not overwhelmed
- Motivated, not guilty

---

## 19. Design Anti‑Goals

Timepad must NOT resemble:

- Employee surveillance software
- Corporate analytics dashboards
- Gamified productivity apps
- Flashy tech interfaces

---

## 20. Brand Personality

If Timepad were a person:

- Thoughtful
- Quietly helpful
- Honest
- Organized but not rigid
- Respectful of personal space

---

## 21. Guiding Principle

Timepad is not about optimizing every minute.\
It is about helping people see their time clearly so they can choose how
to live it.
